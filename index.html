We Cultivate and Grow all Kinds of Trees

In addition to the conifers below, we offer some ORGANIC deciduous trees such as the Populus Tremula, a white poplar hybrid that grows well in the Okanogan.

Austrian Pine

In the United States, Pinus Negra, also known as Austrian pine is mainly planted for shelterbelts, as a street tree, and as an ornamental.

Scots Pine

Pinus Sylvestris, also known as Scots pine makes a great Christmas tree! Approximately 30 percent of all trees planted are for that holiday season.

Ponderosa Pine

Ponderosa pine is widely adapted to a variety of environments from dry, desert-like mesas and plateaus to wet bottomlands. It is one of the most drought-tolerant tree species in North America.

Blue Spruce

Blue spruce are planted extensively as an ornamental in North America and Europe. They are also a popuar Christmas tree.

Norway Spruce

Norway Spruce is recommended for shelterbelt plantings in humid, severe-winter regions. It is also widely planted for Christmas trees and as an ornamental.

Douglas Fir

Douglas Fir is a popular tree in the Okanogan National Forest. The oldest accurately-dated Rocky Mountain Douglas-fir was 930 years old!
